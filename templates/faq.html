Frequently Asked Questions

Applying for an House

Questions related to the process of buying a House under a LLC

Will applying impact my credit score?

For the initial pre-qualification, no. Based on the information you provide, we will modelize your application and give you indicative rates on how much you could buy a house for.

When you buy the house, Fundation will only do a soft credit check, which won’t affect your score.

Do I still need a 20% downpayment?

We accept application with a minimum of 10% downpayment. A higher downpayment will strengthen your application (and decrease your overall month to month cost).

Why buying under a LLC?

A LLC is the legal setup that matches best owning a real estate property with several people. It is separate from anyone specific and allow a lot of flexibility to set the ownership and management rules of your choosing.

Why Fundation is buying the house, and transferring it at the end?

When you get accepted and extend an offer on a house, Fundation will buy the house and enter in a lease agreement with you. This agreement will spread the cost of the house over 27 years. At the end of that period, you will have fully bought your property and Fundation will transfer the house to your own LLC, taking care of setting it up. No extra cost at the end of that period.

Managing your LLC

Questions related to the managment of you LLC

What if one of the owner is not paying?

As a group of owner, you are all signing towards a monthly cost to buy the house. One common way to split that cost is to set a monthly rent per owner and for everyone to be responsible of their share. Bumps do happen though, and if for a month someone is not able to cover their share, others in the house will be required to do so. Fundation is built in a way that if someone is paying more (or covering for someone’s else), their share in the house ownership will increase proportionally to their increased payment.
Example:

If other owners cannot cover the missing payment, it will be extended to the following month with a penalty of 10% of your monthly payment.
After 3 months of missing payment, you will be evicted from the house.

What if we want to sell?

Fundation introduces the notion of lease. After the first two years, and every subsequent year, owners can extend their leases, or not.
If you do not want to extend your lease, then it’s time to sell. You will be able to work with any broker of your choice, or work with us to sell the house at the price of your choice.
When you close your sell, Fundation will close the loan with the initial fund from the sale and send your way any additional gains on the sale.

What are Fundation leases?

Our leases are assets that define how you would like to manage your house. They are mainly used to clarify everything between the owners - who’s paying what, how shares of the house are spread, who lives in or rent it… Fundation store the lease and receive payments, as a 3rd party, to be that non-biaised stakeholder in the house mix.
The initial lease is for a duration of 2 years, and can be extended every year after that.
We have seen three scenarios when renewing a lease:
Keep things as is
Redefine payments and ownership (someone is able to chip in more and interested to acquire more of the house)
Not renew, and proceed to the sale of the house

Is Fundation a loan?

No. Fundaton is not a lender and you are not getting a mortgage.

What if only one person wants to cash out?

At the end of a lease, if someone wants out, Fundation will define their share of the house based on the payments that have been made to date. At that point, we will follow the sequence:
other owners will have the ability to buy that share back at the established price by Fundation, based on % of ownership and estimated house value
the owner who wants to leave will be able to offer their share to external people interested to join-in, but other owners will have a right of first refusal
If none of these options are successful, the lease will be discontinued and the house will be for sale

What about closing costs?

We have integrated most of the services required to close on a house, which allows us to provide a no-surprise, low fee at the time of closing of 2% (~ half of the market price).
